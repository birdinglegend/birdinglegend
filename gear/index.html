How I Think About Gear

I aim for reliable, field‑friendly equipment: fast setup, strong battery life, and weather resilience. Below you’ll find my core kit, what I pack for birding-first days vs filming-first days, and the accessories that keep everything running smoothly.

Cameras

Main bodies for documentary-style birding films and B‑roll.

Sony A7S III

Primary

Shot in S‑Log3 / S‑Gamut3.Cine, 50p (100p for slow‑mo). My main camera for low‑light and flexible color.

  • XAVC HS 4K
  • Prefers native Sony lenses (TBD list)
  • Graded in DaVinci Resolve

GoPro Hero 9

Action / POV

GoPro Flat profile for walk‑throughs, vehicle POV, and quick inserts. I match it to Sony in Resolve.

  • Flat color profile
  • Stabilized POV & time‑savers

Drone

Aerial establishing shots and habitat context.

DJI Air 2S

Aerial

Usually Normal profile at 25p with Auto WB; I sometimes use D‑Log for specific scenes.

  • 1" sensor; crisp aerial cutaways
  • Keep spare ND filters and batteries

Future Upgrade

Audio

Sennheiser MKE 400

On‑Camera

Compact shotgun for run‑and‑gun narration and ambient bush audio.

Support
